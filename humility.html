Pursuing Skills







































With the pursuit of knowledge or skills, people measure themselves against others within their
community of interest. Often dwelling on the ability of others creates two outcomes, either a rat
race to the bottom or stagnation. The former is caused by people looking at an end product before
they have begun and believing that they should be better than that. This notion is detrimental,
while their are people that are naturally talented in specific areas, through dedication most can
become skilled in the area they desire, for others it just might take longer or be less natural. But
     if they study hard and believe in their ability to reach their goals, they will learn
     and grow. The latter is caused by a superiority complex. They think that they have
      made it and therefore that their growth is done. This is a poisonous attitude.
     People should be continually looking to grow and develope. Simply, you will never
      be the best in the world at that skill, accept it and move on. Don't have a
     mindset of being the absolute best, instead enjoy the process, learn the
     skills, find passion, because in the end that will bring you more joy.
      It will be an endless struggle, so you should revel in it.
