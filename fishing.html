Fishing Stories

Fishing has always been a peaceful activity for me. When I was younger I would travel to an old lake house on a tiny private hunting club pond and fish with my brother and grandfather. Some days the water would be so full of algae you could hardly reel in the line without catching a "lake monster". The days were hot and muggy in the summer and often you would stand in the shad of a tree as you waited for a bite that never came. Even the fish couldn't stand the heat. Other days the air would be wet and muggy, with overcast skys and muddy banks. The rain would start to fall just as you were pulling the bass and catfish out of the pond like fish in a barrel. Sometimes we went up to the lake in the fall or winter and fished amid the gray and brown hues. Once my brother fished from one of the small
    decaying docks located in the far shore of the pond and cast and
    somehow fell into the freezing water with one hand holding the edge of
     the dock and other hand holding his pole. My grandfather rushed over
     to save him and after pulling him out and getting him some oversized dry clothes told him he was a great fisherman because he didn't lose the pole. My grandfather grew up in rural Missouri, he loved to fish. I remember he told us that his favorite fishing story when he was a kid was getting a new pole with a reel. Before he had only fished with a cane pole and so when he got a fish on his brand new reeled fishing pole he didn't reel the fish in but ran back up onto the bank like it was a cane pole. He also told us about making a cane pole when he was a boy and catching two bluegill on either side of a slice of apple that he had placed on hisi
     homemade thorn hook. My grandfather even in his seventies was
     proud of that fact. The lake and fishing were very interwined, during
     my childhood 90% of the times I fished I fished at that small pond.
    After fishing or during an interlude I would try to catch frogs, I couldn't catch the huge bullfrogs, but in the spring these tiny baby frogs came out and I tried to grab them and catch them by the handful. while many slipped through my fingers eventually I caught one and then lost in the car on the way home. I never did figure out where the frog ended up. Probably in frog heaven with the heat.
     I also remember walking the hunting loop with friends and family
     and collecting the rusted out shotgun shells that they used to hunt the
     ducks with. Going up to the lake was a magical place, unforuntely as
    I got older trips got less frequent and I got more busy. This year
 before leaving for college I got to go up their one more time, it felt like a farewell
 as any other to my childhood. Someday I will fish again.
