In Defense of Folk Music

I find folk music charming. It seems a world different than       modern pop or rap and seems to speak to my soul. I think this    interests orignates from a minor obession with the past and      people's perceived happiness back then. The songs are simple, and rough much like life over a century ago. These songs create      a certain nostalgia for the simpler time. This longing for the past      is actually not new as seen in the Song Cottage by the Sea       sung by the folk singer Frank Fairfield. But in many ways the       2000's and beyond seem different. Pop music and rap is dominant and the content of these songs seems extremely vapid. Even country which is a direct descendant of folk, just focuses on a few topics: alcohol, tractors, dogs, and romance. Older Folk instead feel more human. It's subject matter is war, romance, moral issues, serial
       killers, fear, absurdity, and many more. On the whole folk
      music tries to capture the varied experience of life. The evils,the
       evil and goodness, the random occurences. Modern music just
      seems to capture the monotonous angst of people that merely want a distraction from the world. Folk songs feel like some sort of stimulate they increase your mood, and give to a kind of respect for the chaotic nature of life. The content of a folk song might include a murder by a lover, but it has a catchy tune, and seems nature given the circumstances of the song. A rap song might talk about a person        getting wasted or high at a party and how they are living the life    , but it doesn't feel real. The content of modern music seems       stuck between two modes partying and short term pleasure(i.e. modern romance) and angst related to not being happy. In many ways these songs represent the cultural experience of many of our generation. The songs feel desperate they don't feel joyful or like a full expression of real life. While trying to provide an escape from modernity it appears to serve the same beliefs and experiences. Folk instead serves reality in a digestable way
     to the audience. It pairs evil(thematically dark songs) often with
     upbeat music, and upbeat songs often with realistic twists. The
     songs all have a sort of dialogue, a story. They have a defined purpose, or defined lack of purpose. Most modern songs don't seem to have that. Those that do have purpose, often tell bland and shallow stories or at least less nuanced and open stories. Overall I enjoy this genre because it tries to connect to the human spirit and human experience. I find it soul pleasing.
